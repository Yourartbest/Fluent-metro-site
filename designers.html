Designers & Influences

The people who shaped Fluent Design and Metro

Fluent Design and Metro didn't emerge in a vacuum. They're the work of talented designers building on decades of modernist design tradition, adapting Swiss principles for the digital age.

Key Designers

Albert Shum

Corporate Vice President of Design, Microsoft

Metro Design Lead (2008-2012)

Albert Shum led the team that created the original Metro design language for Windows Phone 7. His vision of "content before chrome" revolutionized mobile interface design and challenged Apple's skeuomorphic iOS dominance.

Before joining Microsoft, Shum worked at Zune (Microsoft's media player division), where he began developing principles that would become Metro. His background in industrial design influenced Metro's honest, functional aesthetic.

Shum's philosophy: "Design should be authentic to the medium. Digital interfaces shouldn't pretend to be physical objects. They should embrace what screens can do."

Key Contributions:

  • Windows Phone 7 UI: Original Metro design language with Live Tiles, dramatic typography, and content-first philosophy
  • Metro Design Principles: Codified "content before chrome," typography dominance, and authentically digital aesthetics
  • Live Tiles Concept: Dynamic, information-dense tiles showing real-time updates
  • Wayfinding Inspiration: Applied airport signage clarity to mobile UI
"We wanted to create something that felt fast, modern, and uniquely digital. Typography became our primary tool—it's universal, it scales, and it's honest."
— Albert Shum, on Metro's typography-first approach

Windows Phone 7: Albert Shum's Metro vision realized

Microsoft Design team collaboration

Microsoft Design Team

Fluent Design System Creators

2017-Present

The Microsoft Design team evolved Metro into Fluent Design System, adding light, depth, motion, material, and scale as core principles. This team includes hundreds of designers across product divisions.

Key members include Jon Friedman (Corporate Vice President of Design and Research), who leads Fluent's evolution, and numerous designers working on Windows, Office, Xbox, and Azure.

Key Contributions:

  • Fluent Design System (2017): Added light, depth, motion, material, scale to Metro foundation
  • Acrylic Material: Frosted glass effect with blur and noise texture
  • Windows 11 UI (2021): Rounded corners, Mica material, refined Fluent aesthetic
  • Microsoft 365 Redesign: Consistent Fluent across Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams
  • Cross-Platform Fluent: Web, Windows, iOS, Android consistency
"Fluent is about creating experiences that feel natural across all your devices—from your phone to your desktop to your Xbox."
— Jon Friedman, Corporate VP of Design & Research

Design Influences & Inspiration

Metro and Fluent draw from decades of modernist design tradition, particularly Swiss Design and Bauhaus principles.

Josef Müller-Brockmann

1914-1996

Swiss International Style

Pioneer of grid-based design and typographic hierarchy. His mathematical grid systems directly influenced Metro's tile layouts.

Principles Applied to Metro/Fluent:

  • Grid Systems: Metro's 12-column grid and tile alignment
  • Typography as Structure: Large type creates hierarchy
  • Objective Communication: Clear, functional information design
  • Mathematical Precision: Exact spacing and proportions

Notable Works:

  • "Grid Systems in Graphic Design" (1981) — bible of grid-based layout
  • Concert posters for Zürich Tonhalle (1950s-60s)
  • Swiss International Style codification

Massimo Vignelli

1931-2014

Modernist Design / Corporate Identity

Renowned for NYC Subway map and systematic brand design. His rational approach to design systems influenced Microsoft's design thinking.

Principles Applied to Metro/Fluent:

  • Systematic Design: Consistent design tokens and variables
  • Timeless Simplicity: Avoid trends, embrace clarity
  • Limited Typefaces: Segoe UI across all Microsoft products
  • Semantic Color: Colors have meaning and function

Notable Works:

  • NYC Subway map (1972) — wayfinding clarity
  • American Airlines identity (1967)
  • "The Vignelli Canon" (2010) — design philosophy

Bauhaus Movement

1919-1933

Modernist Art & Design School

German art school that united crafts, fine arts, and technology. "Form follows function" became modernism's mantra.

Principles Applied to Metro/Fluent:

  • Form Follows Function: Every UI element serves purpose
  • Honest Materials: Authentically digital (no skeuomorphism)
  • Geometric Shapes: Rectangles, squares, circles dominate
  • Primary Colors: Bold, pure color palette

Key Figures:

  • Walter Gropius (founder) — industrial design integration
  • László Moholy-Nagy — typography experiments
  • Herbert Bayer — universal typeface design

Airport Wayfinding Systems

1970s-Present

Environmental Graphic Design

International airport signage prioritizes instant recognition and clarity across languages and cultures. Direct inspiration for Metro.

Principles Applied to Metro/Fluent:

  • Universal Clarity: Understandable without explanation
  • High Contrast: Readable from distance
  • Iconic Simplicity: Minimal symbols communicate maximum meaning
  • Information Hierarchy: Most important info largest/first

Examples:

  • Heathrow Airport (UK) — Kinneir Calvert system
  • Schiphol Airport (Netherlands) — Benno Wissing design
  • JFK Terminal 4 — consistent wayfinding

Contemporary Metro/Fluent Practitioners

Designers and developers who've embraced and extended Metro/Fluent principles beyond Microsoft.

Web Design Community

After Windows 8 launch, web designers adopted Metro's tile layouts, flat colors, and typography-first approach.

Notable Examples:

  • The Verge (2011 redesign): Tile-based layout, bold typography
  • Chrome Web Store: Colorful tiles inspired by Metro
  • Snowbird: Twitter client with pure Metro aesthetic
  • MetroTwit: Windows Twitter app embracing Metro fully

App Developers

Windows Phone and Windows 8 developers created thousands of apps following Metro guidelines.

Design Characteristics:

  • Panorama views (horizontal scrolling sections)
  • Live Tiles showing dynamic content
  • Full-screen content experiences
  • Segoe UI typography throughout
  • Accent color theming

Design System Teams

Companies building design systems often reference Fluent's approach to tokens, components, and scalability.

Influenced Systems:

  • IBM Carbon: Typography-first, systematic tokens
  • Atlassian Design System: Component consistency
  • Shopify Polaris: Clear hierarchy, functional design
  • Adobe Spectrum: Cross-platform consistency

Metro's Lasting Impact

Industry-Wide Flat Design

Metro's 2010 launch predated Apple's iOS 7 (2013) flat design by three years. Microsoft's bold rejection of skeuomorphism paved the way for the entire industry to embrace authentically digital aesthetics.

Typography as UI Element

Before Metro, large typography was for marketing sites. Metro proved that dramatically large text (72-96px) could be functional UI, not just decoration. This influenced countless design systems.

Component-Based Design

Metro's systematic approach—defined tiles, buttons, navigation patterns—influenced the rise of design systems and component libraries (React, Vue, Angular components).

Content-First Philosophy

"Content before chrome" became a rallying cry beyond Microsoft. Mobile-first design, minimal UI, and progressive disclosure all echo Metro's principles.
